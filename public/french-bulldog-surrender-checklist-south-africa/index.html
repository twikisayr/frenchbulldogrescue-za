French Bulldog surrender checklist for South African owners

If you need help rehoming your Frenchie, the safest move is to prepare a few key details before you submit the form. This usually leads to faster, cleaner triage and fewer delays.

The 7-point checklist before you submit

1. Your contact details

Have your name, mobile number, province, and preferred contact method ready. That is enough to start.

2. Urgency level

Be clear whether this is urgent today, needs help this week, or is planning ahead. Honest urgency improves triage.

3. Basic dog profile

Age, sex, sterilisation status, and whether the dog is microchipped help rescuers assess likely next steps.

4. Medical issues

Note breathing, heat, skin, spine, allergy, mobility, or other known concerns. Frenchies need breed-aware handling.

5. Behaviour summary

Say how your dog is with adults, children, dogs, cats, travel, and handling. A short honest summary is better than a vague one.

6. Why you need help now

Explain the reason briefly, for example relocation, finances, housing, conflict with other pets, or medical strain.

7. Immediate risks

If your Frenchie is unsafe where they are, or struggling medically, say so clearly in the notes and contact a vet first if needed.

Minimum details if you are overwhelmed

You do not need a perfect application.

If you are stressed, submit with just your name, mobile number, province, urgency, and a short description of the situation. The team can collect extra detail afterwards.

What speeds up triage

  • Clear province and nearest major area
  • Honest medical history, especially breathing and heat risk
  • A simple behaviour summary, including children and other pets
  • Whether the dog must leave today, this week, or later
  • Photos ready to share privately if requested after first contact

The goal is not to judge you. It is to match the dog to the safest practical pathway faster.

If your Frenchie has a medical red flag

Breathing distress, overheating, collapse, or severe weakness should be treated as urgent vet problems first. French Bulldogs are a brachycephalic breed, which means airway and heat stress can escalate quickly.

Check the breathing and heat emergency guide

What happens after you submit

Review

The team checks urgency, location, and breed-specific needs.

Contact

You get practical next-step communication, usually same day for urgent cases or within 24 hours otherwise.

Placement planning

Handover, foster, or transfer options are worked through based on safety and capacity.
