What happens when you surrender a Frenchie to us

1

Submit the form

Tell us about your Frenchie, your situation, and what kind of help you need. This gives the rescue team the context they need to act fast.

2

We review and contact you

We assess urgency, breed-specific health needs (BOAS, skin, spine), behaviour, and location. Someone will reach out with clear next steps.

3

Foster placement

Never a kennel. Your Frenchie goes to a vetted foster family who understands brachycephalic needs, skin care, and their individual temperament.

4

Careful matching & adoption

Your Frenchie is matched to a screened home based on their specific health needs and personality — not first-come, first-served.

Common reasons owners need to surrender a French Bulldog

These are the situations we hear about most often. If any sound familiar, you are not the first person to face this — and you will not be judged for it.

I cannot afford my Frenchie's vet bills — BOAS surgery, skin treatment, or IVDD care

French Bulldogs are expensive dogs to keep healthy. BOAS surgery alone can cost R15,000–R30,000. Skin fold dermatitis needs ongoing treatment. IVDD (spinal disc disease) can mean emergency surgery and months of recovery. If you are facing costs you cannot manage, reach out. We do not judge — we help.

I am emigrating or moving and cannot take my Frenchie

Emigration is one of the top reasons Frenchies are surrendered in South Africa. Whether it is a job opportunity overseas, a move to a country with strict breed import rules, or a relocation within SA that does not allow pets — we help plan a safe, unhurried rehoming.

My Frenchie has developed behavioural issues I cannot manage

Separation anxiety, resource guarding, dog reactivity, or aggression can be overwhelming — especially in a breed as stubborn as a Frenchie. Owners often feel ashamed, but behaviour issues are common and manageable with the right environment. Be honest in your form so we can find the right home.

My new rental or complex does not allow pets

South Africa's rental market is tightening. More landlords, body corporates, and estate agents are enforcing no-pet clauses or breed restrictions. If you are moving and cannot take your Frenchie, we understand the bind you are in — and we can help.

My Frenchie has health issues I cannot keep up with

French Bulldogs are prone to allergies, ear infections, skin fold dermatitis, cherry eye, and spinal issues. The ongoing care — special diets, medicated wipes, regular vet visits — adds up. If your Frenchie's health needs have outpaced what you can provide, we can assess and take over their care.

A family member has passed away or gone into care

When an owner passes away or can no longer care for their dog, the family is often left without a plan. We can take in the Frenchie, provide foster care, and find a loving new home — with sensitivity and respect for the situation.

Why surrender through a rescue instead of doing it yourself?

Foster homes, not kennels

Your Frenchie lives with a real family while waiting for adoption. No cages, no shelter stress — especially important for brachycephalic breeds.

Your privacy is protected

Your name, contact details, and personal situation remain confidential. We never share your information with adopters or post it publicly.

Safe from scammers

When you rehome through classifieds or social media groups, your Frenchie can end up with the wrong people — backyard breeders, hoarders, or worse. We screen every adopter thoroughly.
