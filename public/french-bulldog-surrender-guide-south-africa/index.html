French Bulldog surrender guide (South Africa)

If you're here, things are probably stressful. This guide gives you clear steps so your Frenchie stays safe while we work on the best possible outcome.

Step 1: Stabilise your dog's immediate safety

Make sure your Frenchie has water, shade, and a calm space. If your dog is struggling to breathe, overheating, or collapsing, contact your nearest vet immediately. See our emergency help page and breathing and heat guide for urgent breed-specific advice.

Step 2: Gather key details before submitting

  • Dog's age and sex
  • Sterilised or not
  • Known medical history (especially breathing, skin, spine)
  • Behaviour around people, children, and other pets
  • Your location and urgency

Honest information helps us make safer, faster placement decisions.

Step 3: Submit your surrender request

Use the official form so the team can triage properly and respond with next steps.

Start surrender request

Everything you share stays confidential. No judgement — just practical help for your Frenchie.

What the rescue process usually looks like

After submission, the team reviews urgency, breed-specific medical concerns, and location. From there, they work toward the safest available foster or handover pathway.
