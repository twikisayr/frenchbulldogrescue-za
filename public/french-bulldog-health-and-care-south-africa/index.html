French Bulldog health and care basics (South Africa)

Frenchies are wonderful dogs, but they need careful management in our climate. Here are the essentials every owner should know.

Heat and breathing risk

French Bulldogs can overheat quickly, especially in hot and humid weather. Avoid hard exercise in midday heat. Use early morning or evening walks, and always provide cool water and shade.

Urgent warning signs: loud breathing at rest, blue/pale gums, collapse, or inability to settle. Contact a vet immediately.

Skin, ears, and folds

Check skin folds regularly. Keep folds clean and dry. If there is redness, smell, discharge, or constant scratching, book a vet check before it worsens.

Weight and joints

Extra weight puts pressure on breathing and joints. Keep portions consistent and avoid frequent high-calorie treats. Ask your vet for a target weight range for your specific dog.
