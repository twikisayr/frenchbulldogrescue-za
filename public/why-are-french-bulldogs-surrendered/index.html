Why are French Bulldogs surrendered?

If you are considering surrendering your French Bulldog, you are not alone. Understanding the most common reasons other owners reach this point can help you decide what to do next. The decision is difficult, but confidential, judgement-free help is available.

Start confidential surrender request Read surrender guide

Lifestyle and housing changes

This is one of the most frequent reasons French Bulldogs are surrendered in South Africa. Owners face relocation, a new landlord who does not allow pets, or a change in work hours that makes dog care unsustainable. French Bulldogs are companion dogs that struggle with long hours alone, so a sudden lifestyle shift can make ownership feel impossible. If you are in this situation, reach out before the pressure builds — rescue teams can often help plan a safe transition.

Veterinary and health care costs

French Bulldogs are expensive to keep healthy. Between recurring skin and ear infections, breathing surgeries (BOAS correction), spinal issues, allergy treatment, and emergency heat-stress visits, vet bills can run into tens of thousands of rands. Many owners simply cannot keep up. If health costs are the reason you are considering surrender, be honest about the situation in your request — it helps rescue teams understand your Frenchie's medical needs from the start.

See our guide on the cost of a French Bulldog in South Africa for a full breakdown of typical expenses.

Behaviour problems

Growling, resource guarding, dog aggression, or biting are difficult realities that some Frenchie owners face. These behaviours are often rooted in pain, fear, or poor socialisation rather than a "bad dog." In many cases, a vet check and behaviour assessment can make a significant difference. If behaviour is the issue, read our behaviour and surrender guide and include full details in your request so the team can plan accordingly.

Relationship breakdown

Separation, divorce, or the end of a partnership is a common reason dogs are surrendered. When a relationship ends, neither party may be able to take the dog, or the dog may become a point of conflict. Rescue support can step in to ensure the Frenchie lands safely while both parties move forward.

Owner health or incapacity

Serious illness, hospitalisation, or age-related decline can leave owners unable to care for their French Bulldog. In these situations, surrender is not about failing the dog — it is about recognising that the dog's needs have outgrown what the owner can provide. Rescue teams treat these cases with particular care and sensitivity.

Not coping with the breed's needs

French Bulldogs are not low-maintenance dogs. Their breathing issues, heat sensitivity, stubborn temperament, separation anxiety, and high veterinary needs surprise many first-time owners. Some people simply did not know what they were getting into. That is common, and there is no shame in recognising it. The responsible step is to find a safer situation for the dog rather than letting the situation deteriorate.

What to do if any of these apply to you

If you recognise your situation in any of the reasons above, the next step is straightforward. Submit a confidential surrender request. It takes about two minutes. Your details stay private. Urgent cases are prioritised same day where possible, and non-urgent requests usually get a response within 24 hours.
